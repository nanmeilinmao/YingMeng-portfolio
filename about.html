About Me

I'm a graphic design student currently building my practice through print and digital projects. My work is often shaped by an interest in visual storytelling, culture, music, and atmosphere. I enjoy exploring how typography, image, and composition can come together to communicate an idea clearly while also creating a strong mood. As I continue learning, I'm developing my skills in branding, poster design, layout, and image-making, and I'm especially drawn to projects that balance structure with expression.


Behind the Work

Behind my work is a strong interest in mood, narrative, and visual communication. I'm drawn to design that not only looks engaging, but also feels intentional and meaningful. Many of my projects begin with a concept, a story, or a cultural reference, and I enjoy the process of turning those ideas into clear visual forms. Through typography, composition, and color, I aim to create work that feels both thoughtful and distinctive.


My Design Practice

My design practice is centered on learning, experimentation, and concept-driven thinking. I'm interested in how design can shape emotion, communicate ideas, and create memorable visual experiences. Through school projects, I've been developing skills in editorial design, branding, poster design, and digital image editing. I enjoy building visual systems that feel cohesive and expressive, while still maintaining clarity and purpose.


What Shapes My Work

My work is shaped by an interest in storytelling, cultural themes, music, and atmosphere. I'm especially interested in projects that combine visual clarity with a strong emotional tone. Whether I'm working on a poster, a brand identity, or a layout project, I like to think carefully about how color, type, and image can work together to create a clear and engaging visual voice. I see design as a way to make ideas visible in a thoughtful and memorable form.


My Creative Approach

My creative approach begins with observation, research, and concept development. I like starting from an idea, theme, or atmosphere, and then gradually shaping it into a visual language through type, composition, and color. I'm especially drawn to work that feels structured but not rigid, expressive but still clear. For me, design is not only about aesthetics, but also about creating a visual experience that communicates effectively and leaves a lasting impression.
